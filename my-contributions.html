A BUILDER IS BORN!
Neighbours report unusually high curiosity levels — experts predict "this one is going to take everything apart."

Somewhere in India, 1992. — In a year when the world was still dialling up, a small human arrived with an unusual fascination for how things work. First words came late; first questions never stopped.

Witnesses describe a childhood of dismantled toys, rescued screws and suspiciously quiet afternoons. "Every radio in this house has been opened at least twice," one family member reported. "Some of them still work."

That curiosity, sources confirm, would soon be pointed at computers — with consequences this newspaper will document in the pages that follow. Turn to page 3 for the developing story.
